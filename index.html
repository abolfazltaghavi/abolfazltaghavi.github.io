About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Operations Management at UCLA Anderson. My work sits at the intersection of stochastic optimization and learning, with an emphasis on problems where decisions must be made under uncertainty and limited feedback.

A recurring theme in my research is designing policies that are not only theoretically principled, but also implementable at scale: algorithms that translate cleanly into end-to-end pipelines, and models that remain robust when confronted with real operational constraints.

Current focus areas include utility wildfire mitigation, fair and flexible demand response scheduling, and sequential decision-making with uncertainty.

Research

I work on methodological questions—how to learn, optimize, and allocate resources under uncertainty— motivated by operational problems where small improvements can meaningfully change outcomes. Below are a few active projects.

Wildfire Mitigation
Preventing Catastrophic Wildfires: Annual Grid Inspection and Maintenance Plan
with A. Fattahi, S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · Under review, Operations Research
  • Optimization models for inspection/maintenance prioritization under uncertainty.
  • Scalable solution approach designed for large infrastructure networks.
  • Decision support framing: turning risk estimates into actionable annual plans.
Demand Response
Fair Dynamic Load Control Scheduling under Stochastic Energy Demand
with A. Fattahi, S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · In preparation
  • Scheduling framework that balances operational efficiency with fairness across customer groups.
  • Models flexibility in admissible call lengths and quantifies performance tradeoffs.
  • Emphasis on tractability and policy interpretability for utility operations.
Learning & Control
Learning to Prevent Utility-Caused Wildfires through Sequential Inspection and Maintenance
with S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · Working paper
  • Sequential decision-making with noisy signals and evolving system state.
  • Learning-based inspection to reduce catastrophic tail risk over time.
  • Bridges statistical estimation with operational constraints on maintenance actions.
Methods
Stochastic Optimization & Regret-Aware Policies for Infrastructure Decisions
Ongoing
  • Designing policies with explicit guarantees and clear operational interpretations.
