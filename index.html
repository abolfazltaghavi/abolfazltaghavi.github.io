About

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. Candidate in Operations Management at UCLA Anderson School of Management. I received my B.Sc. in Industrial Engineering from Sharif University of Technology.

My research focuses on sustainable operations, with particular emphasis on energy management and wildfire risk mitigation. Methodologically, I am interested in large-scale optimization methods for stochastic optimization and reinforcement learning, especially when decisions must be made under uncertainty and limited feedback.

Research

I develop optimization and learning methods for operational decisions in high-stakes sustainability settings. Current and recent work includes:

  • Preventing Catastrophic Wildfires: Annual Grid Inspection and Maintenance Plan (with A. Fattahi, S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · under review, Operations Research) — Optimization models for inspection/maintenance planning under uncertainty, with scalable solution methods for large infrastructure networks.
  • Fair Dynamic Load Control Scheduling under Stochastic Energy Demand (with A. Fattahi, S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · in preparation) — Scheduling frameworks that balance efficiency and fairness across customer groups, while quantifying tradeoffs induced by operational flexibility constraints.
  • Learning to Prevent Utility-Caused Wildfires through Sequential Inspection and Maintenance (with S. Dasu, R. Ahmadi · working paper) — Sequential decision-making with noisy signals and constrained actions; learning-based inspection strategies aimed at reducing catastrophic tail risk over time.
